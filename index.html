AI-Driven Photobioreactors

Our AI-optimized photobioreactor systems revolutionize algal cultivation through intelligent automation and real-time optimization.

AI-optimized light cycles: Machine learning algorithms dynamically adjust LED spectra and intensity to maximize photosynthetic efficiency and biomass production.

Nutrient delivery systems: Automated precision dosing based on real-time sensor feedback ensures optimal growth conditions while minimizing waste.

Growth prediction models: Predictive analytics forecast biomass yield and identify optimal harvest times, enabling efficient production planning.

CRISPR-Based Microfluidic Platforms

Combining CRISPR technology with microfluidics creates powerful tools for rapid diagnostics and precise gene editing applications.

CRISPR gene detection: Ultrasensitive nucleic acid detection using CRISPR-Cas systems for rapid identification of pathogens and genetic variants.

Microfluidic amplification: Integrated isothermal amplification enables sample-to-answer diagnostics without complex thermal cycling equipment.

Point-of-care diagnostics: Portable, user-friendly devices deliver laboratory-quality results in resource-limited settings within minutes.

Automated gene editing: Microfluidic platforms streamline CRISPR knock-in and knock-out workflows for high-throughput functional genomics studies.

Microfluidics Systems

Advanced microfluidic platforms enable precise manipulation of biological samples at the microscale, revolutionizing research and diagnostics.

Miniaturized biological reactions: Perform complex assays using nanoliter volumes, dramatically reducing reagent costs and sample requirements.

High-throughput screening: Parallel processing of thousands of reactions simultaneously, accelerating drug discovery and optimization workflows.

Lab-on-chip automation: Integrated microfluidic devices combine sample preparation, reaction, and detection in a single portable platform.

Ultra-low reagent workflows: Precision fluid control enables experiments with precious or expensive biological materials while maintaining high reproducibility.
